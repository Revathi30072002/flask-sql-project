women's education:


  The reference to 'similar education' found in ancient texts highlights the educational excellence of women of that time. Along with the ancient males, Avvaiyar, Kakkaipatiniyar, Naccellaiyar, Velliveediyar, Perungoppendu, Parimakalir etc. lived a special life by composing poetry. It can be seen in many places in the ancient literature that women were treated with equal rights.


  In the Middle Ages women were enslaved first to their parents, then to their husbands, and finally to their children. Women in the kitchen were despised as to what education was for. This situation lasted until the twentieth century. Is it possible to describe women as house chandelier and not promote enlightened education that removes the inner light? Is this justice? As they were furious. As a result, we see today that women are also getting higher education.


government initiative:


  Realizing that the country can only benefit from female education, our federal government has introduced a rare program called 'Education for All' and is taking full measures to provide education to all boys and girls between the ages of 6 and 14 years. Provides special incentives for workers' children to study. Our government is providing special incentives for the education of the most backward girls. Provides free bicycles to all students in higher education. Provides free education to all students until graduation. An order has been issued to appoint women teachers in primary schools. Provides marriage grants for students up to eighth grade. Thus, the government is working diligently in various ways to improve women's education.

Conclusion:


  The female community, which was oppressed by the ruling class, is being revived by education. The demand for legislation to elect one-third female members of the legislature and parliament is growing. Women have set up self-help groups and are working with awareness. The situation has developed that only female education can cook a new world. We appreciate female education, remembering that the Greek genius Pythagoras, who lived many centuries ago, stated in his book that men and women should be treated equally in society; Let's create a new world.
