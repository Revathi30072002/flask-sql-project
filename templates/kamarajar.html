Introduction:


  He was called lovingly as 'Southern Gandhi' by all the people of TamilNadu. 'Karma Veerar' is one of the leaders who consider the civilians. He was born in a poor family but became the great leader of Tamil Nadu.


Birth and Youth:


  Kamrajar, who was hailed by Periyar as a miracle man who did not appear in the last 2000 years, was born on July 13, 1903 in Virudhunagar in southern TamilNadu as a child of Kumaraswamy Nadar and Sivagami Ammal. The school life of Kamarajar, who lost his father came to an end.


  The crop is said to be visisble in the bud. Yes! At the age of 17, Kamraj decided to becom a Gandhian volunteer and a full-time employee of the Congress to change the history of the country. Knowing this, the family will arranged him for many jobs. But he said,"My job is to work for the country and dedicate myself to public". He dedicated himself to the motherland by telling his mother that she had to forget him if she foced him to get married when she arrange the wedding thinking that h would change his mind if he got married.


Liberation War life:


  Frequent visits to political meeting in the neghborhood with friends began to ignite the fire of liberation in Kamarajar's heart. The Rowlatt Act, which robbed Indians of their speech and writing, and susequent Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919, aroused the spirit of struggle in him. In 1922, Kamaraj, who was involved in the boycott of the foreign goods, shouted "Tie the knot and drive out the white man".In 1923, he was involved in a toddy shop riot in Madhurai. Participated in the Nagapur Flag struggle and was sentenced to two years imprisonment for participating in the Salt Satyagraha war in Vedaranyam in 1930. In 1931, Kamaraj was released due to Gandhi's Irwin agreement. In 1933, Kamaraj was sent to jail in conspiracy case but later released becase he was not convicted. He was imprisoned in Amaravati in 1942 for participating in the 'Get Out White' protest and was sentence to 3 years in prison. Release in 1945. Thus he was imprisoned for about 9 years and made struggle as his life.


Chief minister Kamaraj:


  As the privelege of Tamil Nadu, he was appointed asChief Minister of TamilNadu in April 8, 1954 and the clan planned to impact the highly affected poor people. So it's the Oppostive team that appreciated his as 'Best Tamilan'. He implementate free uniform for students to avoid the caste differentiation among the students. He offered free education until the high school. For the poor students he introduced the free lunch plan, so that they can come and study. To not have a school-free town, he opened up 22,000 schoolls and hundres of colleges in their regime and he was praised as 'Education Eye Opener'. Many program plans such as factories, dams and industries are opened. For the compromised elderly, he introduced the pension plan. He abolished drinks and bribes. He reduced the spendings of Government and multiplied the savings which changed Tamil Nadu as predecessor state.


the great leader Kamaraj:


  "The leader of the leader Kamarajar", says poet Vairamuthu. It's the truth. He said that Not only did the elders in the party should retire themselves but to hire young men for the party. He himself retired from the party and became the role model for others. When the country's hight post Prime Minister had been competitive he discussed with all the party members and identified elegible individuals qualified for the post. So he was praised as "King Maker". Despite other leaders in the country, Kamrajar was the great leader.


Characteristics:


  The Kamrajar desires simple life; he has no desire for power; He equally considered the blame and praise; He didn't even gave his mother extra care by taking advantage of his power; Book favor; Unchanged in policy; loved motherland more than his mother. Less speech, more act is a frozen feeling in the heart of Kamraj.


Conclusion:


  By the calling of his dear leader Gandhiji, he workded day and night for the mother land. Lately, in his 73rd birthday in october, 1975 he passed away on his favourite leader Gandhiji's birthday.


Long live Gandhi's name! Grow Kamraj's fame!
