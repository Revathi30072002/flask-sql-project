Birth and Youth:



  Dr.A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931 in Rameswaram, on the east coast of Tamil Nadu, the son of Jainulapteen and Ashiamma. Kalam graduated from Rameswaram Schwartz High School and went to Trichy to complete his college studies. He then obtained an engineering degree in aviation from the Chennai Institute of Technology.


Research Work:


  Abdul Kalam worked in the Department of Military Research and Development and in the Indian Space Research Organization. He was also the mastermind behind missile production and satellite launches. Abdul Kalam was the project director of SLV-3, India's first rocket. That is how the Rohini satellite was launched. While serving in the military, he laid the foundation for India to develop its own missiles. From 1992 to 1999 he served as Minister of Defense and Scientific Adviser to the Department of Military Research. From 1999 to 2001 he served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to India. He later became a professor at Anna University, Chennai.

Awards Received:



  Universities and educational institutions around the world have awarded 30 honorary doctorates in recognition of Abdul Kalam's service in the field of science. Our Government of India awarded him the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990. In 1997, he was awarded India's highest award, the Bharat Ratna. Abdul Kalam was then elected the 12th President of India and did good deeds as the first citizen of the country.
